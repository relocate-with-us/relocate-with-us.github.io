      <h2>1. How the UK Skilled Worker Visa Works</h2>
      <p>The Skilled Worker visa requires three things: a job offer from a licensed UK sponsor, a role that meets the skill and salary thresholds, and sufficient English language proficiency.</p>

      <h3>Key requirements</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Job offer from a licensed sponsor:</strong> The employer must hold a valid Skilled Worker sponsor licence issued by the Home Office</li>
        <li><strong>Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS):</strong> The employer generates this document and assigns it to you. It confirms the job details, salary, and sponsor information</li>
        <li><strong>Salary threshold:</strong> Your salary must meet the minimum for your specific occupation code (Standard Occupational Classification, or SOC code) <em>and</em> the general minimum salary — whichever is higher</li>
        <li><strong>Skill level:</strong> The role must be at or equivalent to A-level or above (RQF Level 3+)</li>
        <li><strong>English language:</strong> B1 level (intermediate) on the Common European Framework</li>
        <li><strong>Maintenance funds:</strong> You must have at least £1,270 in your bank account (waived if your Certificate of Sponsorship confirms your employer will cover this)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. UK Skilled Worker Visa Salary Threshold 2025</h2>
      <p>This is the most important update for 2025. The salary thresholds were significantly increased in April 2024:</p>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>General minimum salary:</strong> <strong>£38,700/year</strong> (increased from £26,200 in April 2024)</li>
        <li><strong>New entrant rate:</strong> £30,960/year (for workers who are new to their career, switching from a student visa, or working in a shortage occupation)</li>
        <li><strong>Health and Care visa:</strong> Separate, lower thresholds apply — see our <a href="/blog/posts/health-care-visa-jobs-uk/" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">Health and Care Visa guide</a></li>
      </ul>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Going rate applies:</strong> Even if you earn above £38,700, your salary must also meet the "going rate" for your specific SOC code. If the going rate for your occupation is higher than £38,700, the going rate applies. Check the <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-going-rates-for-eligible-occupations" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">Home Office going rates table</a> for your specific role.</p>

      <h2>3. How to Find a UK Sponsor Employer</h2>
      <p>This is the central challenge for most international job seekers. Here's how to approach it effectively:</p>

      <h3>Check the Register of Licensed Sponsors</h3>
      <p>The UK Home Office publishes a downloadable list of every organisation that holds a current Skilled Worker sponsor licence. This is your most powerful research tool.</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Download the register from <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-workers" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline font-medium" rel="noopener noreferrer">gov.uk — Register of Licensed Sponsors</a></li>
        <li>The list includes all organisation names, types (employer/education), and ratings (A or B)</li>
        <li>Filter by your industry or company name. If a company isn't on the list, they cannot legally sponsor you</li>
        <li>The list is updated monthly. Always download the most recent version</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>4. Which Industries Sponsor the Most Skilled Worker Visas?</h2>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>🏥 NHS and Healthcare:</strong> The single largest sponsor by volume. The NHS employs hundreds of thousands of internationally recruited professionals. Nurses, doctors, allied health professionals</li>
        <li><strong>💻 Technology:</strong> London's tech ecosystem (and Manchester, Edinburgh, Bristol) regularly sponsor software engineers, data scientists, cybersecurity professionals</li>
        <li><strong>🏗️ Engineering and Infrastructure:</strong> Civil, structural, mechanical engineers for infrastructure and construction projects</li>
        <li><strong>🎓 Education:</strong> Universities and secondary schools sponsor academics and secondary school teachers in shortage subjects</li>
        <li><strong>💼 Finance:</strong> London's financial services sector sponsors banking, insurance, and FinTech professionals</li>
        <li><strong>⚕️ Social care:</strong> The Health and Care visa covers a broader range of roles — see our dedicated guide</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>5. Engineering Jobs in the UK on the Skilled Worker Visa</h2>
      <p>Engineering is one of the strongest sectors for UK visa sponsorship. Key roles and their SOC codes:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Civil engineers</strong> (SOC 2121) — Going rate ~£40,700; major demand on HS2, Thames Tideway, and nuclear projects</li>
        <li><strong>Electrical engineers</strong> (SOC 2123) — Grid decarbonisation and offshore wind creating significant demand</li>
        <li><strong>Mechanical engineers</strong> (SOC 2122) — Aerospace, defence, and manufacturing</li>
        <li><strong>Software engineers</strong> (SOC 2136) — London tech hub; also Edinburgh, Bristol, Manchester</li>
        <li><strong>Chemical engineers</strong> (SOC 2124) — Pharma, oil and gas, chemicals</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>6. Costs and Timeline</h2>
      <h3>Costs for the applicant</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Visa application fee:</strong> £827 (up to 3 years) or £1,636 (more than 3 years) if applying outside the UK</li>
        <li><strong>Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS):</strong> £1,035 per year. For a 5-year visa: £5,175. This gives you access to NHS healthcare</li>
        <li><strong>Priority service:</strong> Additional £500 for processing within 5 business days (from outside UK)</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Costs for the employer</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Sponsor licence application:</strong> £536 (small/charitable) or £1,476 (medium/large)</li>
        <li><strong>Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) fee:</strong> £239 per CoS</li>
        <li><strong>Immigration Skills Charge:</strong> £364/year (small employer) or £1,000/year (medium/large) — payable for each year of the visa</li>
        <li><strong>Total employer cost per hire:</strong> £3,000–£6,000+ for a 3-year visa</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Processing time</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Standard:</strong> 3–8 weeks from application submission</li>
        <li><strong>Priority:</strong> 5 business days (significant premium, but available)</li>
        <li><strong>Total from job offer to start date:</strong> Budget 2–4 months</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>7. Path to Permanent Residency (ILR)</h2>
      <p>After 5 years on a Skilled Worker visa (or other eligible visas), you can apply for <strong>Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)</strong> — the UK equivalent of permanent residency. Requirements include:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>5 years of continuous lawful residence in the UK</li>
        <li>Passing the <strong>Life in the UK test</strong> (English language and citizenship knowledge)</li>
        <li>Meeting the English language requirement (B1 level)</li>
        <li>Absences from the UK of no more than 180 days in any 12-month period</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>8. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can I switch from a Student visa to a Skilled Worker visa?</h3>
      <p>Yes. If you are currently in the UK on a Student visa and you receive a job offer from a licensed sponsor, you can switch to a Skilled Worker visa without leaving the UK. The "new entrant" salary rate (£30,960) may apply if you are within 3 years of graduating.</p>

      <h3>Can my family come with me on a Skilled Worker visa?</h3>
      <p>Yes. Your spouse/civil partner and dependent children under 18 can apply as dependants. They will receive the right to work and study in the UK. Dependants pay separate visa fees and IHS charges.</p>

      <h3>What is the Shortage Occupation List?</h3>
      <p>The Shortage Occupation List (SOL) previously allowed employers to pay 20% below the standard going rate. As of April 2024, the SOL was replaced by the <strong>Immigration Salary List (ISL)</strong>, but the 20% discount was removed. The ISL still identifies roles where there is a recognised shortage — check the <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-immigration-salary-list" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">current ISL</a> for your occupation.</p>
