      <p>Australia faces a nationwide teacher shortage — the federal government projects a shortfall of 4,000+ secondary school teachers by 2025, particularly in STEM subjects, special education, and rural areas. This has opened the door to a wave of international teacher recruitment, with state education departments and private schools directly sponsoring overseas teachers through the 482 visa program.</p>

      <h2>1. Which Teaching Occupations Qualify for the 482 Visa?</h2>
      <p>Secondary school teachers are on the <strong>MLTSSL (Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List)</strong>, making them eligible for a 4-year visa with a pathway to permanent residency. Primary school teachers are on the STSOL (2-year visa). Here are the key ANZSCO codes:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Secondary School Teacher</strong> (ANZSCO 241411) — MLTSSL. 4-year 482 visa. PR pathway via ENS after 3 years</li>
        <li><strong>Primary School Teacher</strong> (ANZSCO 241213) — STSOL. 2-year 482 visa. No direct PR pathway (but regional route available)</li>
        <li><strong>Special Education Teacher</strong> (ANZSCO 241511) — MLTSSL. 4-year visa</li>
        <li><strong>Early Childhood (Pre-primary school) Teacher</strong> (ANZSCO 241111) — MLTSSL. 4-year visa</li>
      </ul>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Most in demand:</strong> Secondary teachers in Maths, Science, Physics, Chemistry, Computing/IT, Special Education, and Languages are the most sought-after. If you teach any of these subjects, your chances of sponsorship are significantly higher.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>2. Registration Requirements: AITSL and State Teaching Registration</h2>
      <p>To teach in Australia, you need two levels of registration:</p>

      <h3>Step 1: AITSL Assessment</h3>
      <p>The <strong>Australian Institute for Teaching and School Leadership (AITSL)</strong> assesses whether your overseas qualification is comparable to an Australian Initial Teacher Education (ITE) degree.</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Who needs it:</strong> All overseas-qualified teachers applying for sponsored positions</li>
        <li><strong>What they assess:</strong> Your teaching qualification, subject specialisation, and practical teaching hours</li>
        <li><strong>Cost:</strong> AUD $200</li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> 6–10 weeks</li>
        <li><strong>Apply at:</strong> <a href="https://www.aitsl.edu.au/teach/overseas-qualifications" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">AITSL Overseas Qualifications Assessment</a></li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Step 2: State or Territory Teaching Registration</h3>
      <p>Each Australian state and territory has its own teacher registration body. You must register with the body in the state where you'll be working:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>NSW:</strong> NSW Education Standards Authority (NESA)</li>
        <li><strong>VIC:</strong> Victorian Institute of Teaching (VIT)</li>
        <li><strong>QLD:</strong> Queensland College of Teachers (QCT)</li>
        <li><strong>WA:</strong> Teacher Registration Board of Western Australia (TRBWA)</li>
        <li><strong>SA:</strong> Teachers Registration Board of South Australia (TRBSA)</li>
        <li><strong>TAS:</strong> Teachers Registration Board of Tasmania (TRBT)</li>
        <li><strong>ACT:</strong> Teacher Quality Institute (TQI)</li>
        <li><strong>NT:</strong> Teacher Registration Board of the Northern Territory (TRBNT)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>3. English Language Requirements</h2>
      <p>All overseas teachers must meet English proficiency requirements for both AITSL assessment and state registration. Typically:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>IELTS Academic:</strong> 7.5 overall, with no band below 7.0 (some states require 8.0 in writing)</li>
        <li><strong>OET (Occupational English Test):</strong> B grade in all four subtests</li>
        <li><strong>Exemptions:</strong> Citizens and recent graduates from Australia, UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, New Zealand, and South Africa may be exempt — check with the specific state registration body</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>4. Which States Are Recruiting International Teachers?</h2>

      <h3>New South Wales</h3>
      <p>NSW Department of Education runs a dedicated international teacher recruitment program called <strong>Teach NSW</strong>. They recruit teachers from the UK, Ireland, Canada, South Africa, and other countries with equivalent qualifications. They assist with 482 visa sponsorship for hard-to-fill subjects.</p>

      <h3>Queensland</h3>
      <p>Queensland has a <strong>Great State. Great Opportunity.</strong> international teacher recruitment campaign targeting UK, Irish, Canadian, and South African teachers. The Queensland Department of Education handles visa sponsorship.</p>

      <h3>Western Australia</h3>
      <p>WA has the most acute teacher shortage due to its remote communities and mining sector growth. The WA Department of Education recruits internationally with significant salary incentives for regional placements.</p>

      <h3>Victoria</h3>
      <p>The Victorian Department of Education sponsors teachers through the 482 visa, particularly in outer-suburban growth areas and regional Victoria.</p>

        <li><strong>Experienced Teacher (Step 5–6):</strong> AUD $95,000–$105,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Highly Accomplished Teacher:</strong> AUD $110,000–$120,000/year</li>

      <h2>7. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can primary school teachers get a sponsored visa in Australia?</h3>
      <p>Yes, but Primary School Teacher is on the STSOL (2-year visa), not the MLTSSL. For longer-term pathways, consider applying for regional placements where the SESR visa (subclass 494) may apply, or plan to transition to permanent residency through SkillSelect after accumulating Australian work experience.</p>

      <h3>How long does the full process take?</h3>
      <p>Budget 4–8 months from starting your AITSL assessment to arriving in Australia. State registration can often be done concurrently with the 482 visa application. Some state departments make provisional offers before registration is complete.</p>

      <h3>Do I need to take additional subject knowledge tests?</h3>
      <p>Some state registration bodies require overseas teachers to demonstrate subject knowledge through testing or additional coursework, particularly if your qualification is in a different specialty than the role. Check with your target state's registration body.</p>
