      <!-- ==================== 1. BERLIN ==================== -->
      <h2>1. Berlin, Germany</h2>
      <p>Berlin is Europe's startup capital. The city hosts over 3,000 startups and major tech companies like Zalando, Delivery Hero, N26, SoundCloud, and HelloFresh. Most startups operate entirely in English, making it one of the easiest cities in continental Europe for English-speaking professionals. Compared to London or Zurich, the cost of living is significantly lower — though Berlin rents have risen sharply since 2019.</p>
      <p>Germany's <strong>EU Blue Card</strong> is one of the most attractive work permits in Europe: it offers a fast-track path to permanent residency (21 months with B1 German) and allows mobility across the EU.</p>

        <li><strong>Main visa:</strong> Swiss <a href="https://www.sem.admin.ch/sem/en/home/themen/arbeit/nicht-eu_efta-angehoerige.html" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">L-permit or B-permit</a> for non-EU/EFTA nationals. Quotas apply, and the employer must demonstrate they could not fill the role with a local or EU candidate</li>
        <li><strong>Average tech salary:</strong> CHF 100,000–140,000 (€105,000–€147,000) (senior: CHF 140,000–200,000+)<sup><a href="#fn1">[1]</a></sup></li>
        <li><strong>Cost of living:</strong> 1-bed apartment in city centre: CHF 2,000–2,800/month (€2,100–€2,950)<sup><a href="#fn2">[2]</a></sup></li>
        <li><strong>Language:</strong> German (Zurich), but English is used extensively in tech. Some roles in western Switzerland use French</li>
        <li><strong>Official immigration portal:</strong> <a href="https://www.sem.admin.ch/sem/en/home.html" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">State Secretariat for Migration (SEM)</a></li>
      </ul>

      <h2>10. Tallinn, Estonia</h2>
      <p>Tallinn is the birthplace of Skype and home to the world's most digitally advanced government. Estonia's e-Residency programme and Digital Nomad Visa have made it a destination for tech-savvy professionals. The startup ecosystem is young but growing fast, costs are significantly lower than Western Europe, and the government is actively working to attract international tech talent.</p>

      <h3>Job boards for Tallinn</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">

        <li><a href="https://www.cvkeskus.ee/en" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline font-medium">CV Keskus</a> — Estonia's largest job portal (English section available)</li>

      <!-- ==================== HOW TO CHOOSE ==================== -->
      <h2>How to Choose Your Destination</h2>
      <p>Choosing where to move is a personal decision that goes beyond salary. Consider these factors:</p>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Visa accessibility:</strong> How easy is it to get a work permit? Some countries (Germany, Netherlands) have very streamlined processes for tech workers. Others (Switzerland) have quotas and lengthy processes</li>
        <li><strong>Net pay after tax:</strong> A high gross salary can be misleading. Use net salary calculators to compare: <a href="https://www.brutto-netto-rechner.info/" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">Germany</a>, <a href="https://thetax.nl/" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">Netherlands</a>, <a href="https://www.thesalarycalculator.co.uk/" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">UK</a></li>
        <li><strong>Language:</strong> Can you work and live comfortably in English? Berlin and Amsterdam are much easier than Munich or Zurich in this regard</li>
        <li><strong>Career growth:</strong> Is there a strong ecosystem in your specific niche? If you work in fintech, London is hard to beat. If automotive tech, Munich</li>
        <li><strong>Quality of life:</strong> Healthcare quality, safety, public transport, weather, social life, and cultural scene all matter. Use <a href="https://www.numbeo.com/quality-of-life/rankings.jsp" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">Numbeo's Quality of Life Index</a> for objective comparisons</li>
        <li><strong>Path to permanent residency:</strong> If you plan to settle long-term, consider how quickly you can get permanent residency or citizenship. Germany offers PR in as little as 21 months with the Blue Card</li>
      </ul>
