      <h2>1. Construction & Engineering Occupations on the 482 Visa List</h2>
      <p>The following construction-related occupations are on the MLTSSL (Medium-Term stream, 4-year visa, PR pathway):</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Civil Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233211) — Roads, bridges, water infrastructure</li>
        <li><strong>Structural Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233214) — Buildings and frameworks</li>
        <li><strong>Mechanical Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233512) — Plant, equipment, HVAC</li>
        <li><strong>Electrical Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233311) — Power, industrial, construction</li>
        <li><strong>Construction Project Manager</strong> (ANZSCO 133111) — Oversees full project delivery</li>
        <li><strong>Building and Construction Project Manager</strong> — Specific to residential/commercial builds</li>
        <li><strong>Quantity Surveyor</strong> (ANZSCO 233213) — Cost estimation and contract management</li>
        <li><strong>Geotechnical Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233212) — Soil and foundation assessment</li>
        <li><strong>Environmental Engineer</strong> (ANZSCO 233915) — Environmental compliance on sites</li>
        <li><strong>Surveyor</strong> (ANZSCO 232212) — Site layout, boundary, and cadastral</li>
      </ul>

      <p>Skilled trade occupations (typically STSOL, 2-year visa) include:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Electricians, plumbers, carpenters (subject to skills assessment via TRA)</li>
        <li>Bricklayers, concreters, tilers</li>
        <li>Plant and equipment operators (some classifications)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. Skills Assessment: Engineers Australia</h2>
      <p>For professional engineering occupations, the skills assessing body is <strong>Engineers Australia (EA)</strong>. This is mandatory for most engineering visa applications.</p>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Submit your engineering degree, transcripts, and work experience documentation to EA</li>
        <li>EA assesses whether your qualification meets Australian engineering standards at the appropriate level (Professional Engineer, Engineering Associate, or Engineering Technologist)</li>
        <li><strong>Cost:</strong> AUD $780–$1,200 depending on assessment type</li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> 8–16 weeks for standard assessment; 4 weeks for priority processing (higher fee)</li>
        <li><strong>CDR option:</strong> If your degree is not from a Washington Accord signatory country, you may need to submit a <strong>Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)</strong> — a detailed portfolio of engineering work</li>
        <li>Apply at: <a href="https://www.engineersaustralia.org.au/skills-assessment" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">Engineers Australia — Skills Assessment</a></li>
      </ol>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Washington Accord countries:</strong> If your engineering degree is from a Washington Accord signatory country (UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, South Africa, India, South Korea, Japan, and others), your degree is typically accepted without a CDR, making the assessment significantly faster and simpler.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>6. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Do I need to be a chartered engineer to get a sponsored visa?</h3>
      <p>No. Chartered status (CPEng from Engineers Australia) is desirable but not required for the 482 visa. A positive skills assessment from EA is sufficient.</p>

      <h3>Can skilled tradespeople (electricians, plumbers) get sponsored visas?</h3>
      <p>Yes, but trades require a skills assessment through Trades Recognition Australia (TRA), not Engineers Australia. Additionally, Australian states and territories require separate licensing for electricians and plumbers before they can work legally. Mutual Recognition Agreements exist with some countries (UK, Ireland, Canada, NZ), which can expedite the licensing process.</p>

      <h3>What is FIFO work and should I consider it?</h3>
      <p>FIFO (fly-in fly-out) means working at a remote mine or construction site for a roster period (e.g., 2 weeks on, 1 week off) and flying home for your days off. It pays significantly more than metropolitan roles — often 30–50% above city rates — and is an excellent way to save money quickly while building Australian experience and progressing toward permanent residency.</p>
