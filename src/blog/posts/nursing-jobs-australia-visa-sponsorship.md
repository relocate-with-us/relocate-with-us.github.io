      <h2>1. The Visa Pathway for International Nurses</h2>
      <p>Most international nurses enter Australia via the <strong>Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa, subclass 482, Medium-Term stream</strong>. Registered Nursing is on the MLTSSL (Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List), meaning:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>You get a <strong>4-year visa</strong> (not the 2-year short-term stream)</li>
        <li>After 3 years with your employer, you can apply for the <strong>Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS) permanent residency visa</strong></li>
        <li>Your spouse receives full work rights in Australia</li>
        <li>Your employer pays a Skilling Australians Fund levy of AUD $1,800/year</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Nursing occupations on the MLTSSL</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Registered Nurse (General) — ANZSCO 254412</li>
        <li>Registered Nurse (Aged Care) — ANZSCO 254411</li>

      <h2>2. AHPRA Registration: The Essential First Step</h2>
      <p>Before any Australian employer can sponsor you, you must obtain registration with the <strong>Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)</strong> — specifically through the <strong>Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia (NMBA)</strong>.</p>

      <h3>Steps to AHPRA registration</h3>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Check qualification comparability:</strong> Your nursing qualification must be assessed as comparable to Australian standards. AHPRA assesses this as part of the registration process</li>
        <li><strong>English language requirement:</strong> Must achieve required scores on IELTS Academic (7.0 overall, no band below 7.0), OET (B grade in all subtests), or equivalent. Some exemptions apply for candidates from certain English-speaking countries</li>
        <li><strong>Apply online:</strong> Submit your application at <a href="https://www.nursingmidwiferyboard.gov.au/Registration-and-Endorsement/International.aspx" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">NMBA — International Applicants</a></li>
        <li><strong>Provide documentation:</strong> Proof of qualification, identity, professional reference, criminal history declaration, English test results</li>
        <li><strong>Assessment outcome:</strong> You will receive either full registration, conditional registration, or a requirement to complete a supervised practice period</li>
      </ol>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Important:</strong> Processing times for AHPRA registration can be 3–6 months or longer. Start this process before applying for jobs — most Australian employers require AHPRA registration (or a clear pathway to it) before they will proceed with sponsorship. Some large hospital networks will make conditional offers and support you through the registration process.</p>

      <h2>3. Skills Assessment for the 482 Visa</h2>
      <p>For nursing, the <strong>skills assessing body is AHPRA/NMBA</strong>. Your AHPRA registration itself serves as the skills assessment for the 482 visa — you do not need a separate skills assessment from a different body. This streamlines the process considerably compared to other professions.</p>

      <h2>8. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can enrolled nurses (ENs) get a sponsored visa in Australia?</h3>
      <p>Enrolled nurses are not on the MLTSSL, so they cannot get a 482 Medium-Term visa. However, some ENs qualify under regional labour agreements or can upgrade to Registered Nurse (through a bridging program) to access the MLTSSL pathway.</p>

      <h3>Do I need to take the NCLEX or any Australian-specific nursing exam?</h3>
      <p>Australia does not use the NCLEX. Assessment is done through AHPRA/NMBA based on your qualifications and English ability. Depending on your education background, you may be required to complete a supervised practice period in Australia before full registration is granted.</p>

      <h3>Will my employer pay for my AHPRA registration?</h3>
      <p>Many large hospital networks and aged care providers cover or reimburse the AHPRA registration fee as part of their international recruitment offer. Always negotiate this when you receive a job offer — along with flights, temporary accommodation, and orientation support.</p>
