However, significant changes were made in 2024 that affect who can bring dependants — making this a crucial guide for anyone considering this pathway.</p>

      <h2>1. What Is the Health and Care Visa?</h2>
      <p>The Health and Care visa is a sub-category of the Skilled Worker visa, available to workers in eligible healthcare and social care roles. Its key advantages over the standard Skilled Worker visa include:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Reduced visa fees:</strong> Significantly lower application fees than the standard Skilled Worker visa</li>
        <li><strong>No Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS):</strong> Health and Care visa holders are exempt from paying the IHS — a saving of £1,035 per year</li>
        <li><strong>Priority processing:</strong> Applications are processed faster than standard Skilled Worker applications</li>
        <li><strong>Broader employer eligibility:</strong> Covers NHS trusts, NHS-funded bodies, and CQC-regulated social care providers</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. Eligible Roles for the Health and Care Visa</h2>
      <p>The Health and Care visa covers a specific list of occupations. The main eligible roles include:</p>

      <h3>NHS and clinical roles</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Registered Nurses (all specialisations)</li>
        <li>Doctors and Surgeons</li>
        <li>Pharmacists and Pharmacy Technicians</li>
        <li>Radiographers</li>
        <li>Physiotherapists</li>
        <li>Occupational Therapists</li>
        <li>Paramedics</li>
        <li>Health visitors and district nurses</li>
        <li>Midwives</li>
        <li>Social workers</li>
        <li>Medical laboratory technicians</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Social care roles (added from February 2022)</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Senior Care Worker</strong> (SOC 6146) — The most common sponsored social care role</li>
        <li><strong>Care Worker</strong> (SOC 6145) — Subject to restrictions following 2024 changes (see below)</li>
        <li>Home carers, residential care home workers, support workers</li>
      </ul>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">

      <h2>3. The 2024 Rule Changes: What Changed and Who Is Affected</h2>
      <p>In March 2024, the UK government introduced significant restrictions on the Health and Care visa that every applicant must understand:</p>

      <h3>No dependants for care workers (from 11 March 2024)</h3>
      <p>The most impactful change: <strong>care workers and senior care workers applying for a Health and Care visa on or after 11 March 2024 can no longer bring dependants (spouse/partner or children) to the UK.</strong></p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>This restriction applies to new applicants in SOC codes 6145 (care worker) and 6146 (senior care worker)</li>
        <li>Clinical roles (nurses, doctors, physiotherapists, etc.) are <strong>not affected</strong> — they can still bring dependants</li>
        <li>Care workers who were already in the UK before 11 March 2024 may be able to extend and retain dependants — check the <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/immigration-rules/immigration-rules-appendix-health-care-worker" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">current immigration rules</a></li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Tightened employer eligibility</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>From March 2024, social care employers must be <strong>registered with the Care Quality Commission (CQC)</strong> to sponsor Health and Care visa workers</li>
        <li>Employers who are not CQC-registered can no longer sponsor social care workers</li>
        <li>This was aimed at tackling immigration abuse by unscrupulous care employers</li>
      </ul>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Important decision:</strong> If you are a care worker with a family, the dependant restriction is a major consideration. Some people in this situation choose to apply for a standard <strong>Skilled Worker visa</strong> instead (if their salary meets the higher threshold) or to pursue other UK visa routes that still permit dependants. Seek advice from a registered immigration adviser before proceeding.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>4. Salary Requirements for the Health and Care Visa</h2>
      <p>Health and Care visa salary thresholds are set at the lower of:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>The <strong>going rate for the specific SOC code</strong></li>
        <li>The <strong>general threshold</strong> for the Health and Care visa (currently £23,200/year, subject to updates)</li>
      </ul>

      <p>In practice, typical salaries for Health and Care visa roles:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Care Worker:</strong> £22,000–£28,000/year (many are above the visa minimum)</li>
        <li><strong>Senior Care Worker:</strong> £25,000–£32,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Registered Nurse (Band 5, NHS):</strong> £28,407–£34,581/year (Agenda for Change pay scale)</li>
        <li><strong>Registered Nurse (Band 6):</strong> £35,392–£42,618/year</li>
        <li><strong>Specialist Nurse:</strong> £42,618–£50,000+/year</li>
        <li><strong>NHS Doctor (Foundation):</strong> £36,616/year minimum</li>
      </ul>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">

        <li>Most NHS trusts have dedicated international recruitment teams and can process Health and Care visa sponsorship efficiently</li>
        <li>The <strong>NMC (Nursing and Midwifery Council)</strong> registration is required before starting work — begin this process as early as possible as it can take 3–6 months</li>

      <h2>7. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Is the Health and Care visa better than the standard Skilled Worker visa?</h3>
      <p>For eligible healthcare roles, yes — it is cheaper (no IHS surcharge) and processed faster. The trade-off for care workers is the 2024 restriction on dependants. Clinical professionals (nurses, doctors, therapists) are generally not affected by the dependant restriction.</p>

      <h3>Can I switch from a Health and Care visa to a standard Skilled Worker visa?</h3>
      <p>Yes, if you receive a job offer in an eligible role that meets the standard Skilled Worker salary threshold (£38,700). Switching allows you to bring dependants if they could not come on your Health and Care visa.</p>

      <h3>How long does NMC registration take for overseas nurses?</h3>
      <p>Overseas nurse NMC registration typically takes 3–6 months. The process involves a document check, English language verification, and in most cases an Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) in the UK. Many NHS trusts provide OSCE training and support as part of their international recruitment package.</p>
