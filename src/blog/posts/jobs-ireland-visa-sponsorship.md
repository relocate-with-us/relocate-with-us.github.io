      <h2>1. Ireland's Employment Permit System: An Overview</h2>
      <p>Ireland's employment permits are governed by the Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment (DETE). The two main permit types for skilled workers are:</p>

      <h3>Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP)</h3>
      <p>The faster, more prestigious permit for highly skilled roles:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>For occupations on Ireland's Critical Skills Eligible Roles list — predominantly ICT, engineering, science, healthcare, and senior management</li>
        <li>Minimum salary: <strong>€38,000/year</strong> (for roles on the critical skills list) or <strong>€64,000/year</strong> (for roles not on the list but considered critical)</li>
        <li><strong>No labour market test required</strong> for listed occupations — the employer does not need to advertise the role locally first</li>
        <li>After 2 years: Eligible for Stamp 4 (unrestricted work permission — work for any employer)</li>
        <li>After 5 years: Eligible to apply for Irish citizenship (with continuous residence)</li>
        <li>Spouse/partner can apply for a <strong>Stamp 1G</strong> allowing unrestricted work in Ireland</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>General Employment Permit (GEP)</h3>
      <p>Broader in scope but more complex process:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>For roles not on the Critical Skills list and not on the Ineligible Occupations List</li>
        <li>Minimum salary: <strong>€34,000/year</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Labour market test required:</strong> The employer must advertise the role for 4 weeks in Ireland and the EU/EEA before hiring outside the EU</li>
        <li>After 5 years: Eligible for Stamp 4</li>
        <li>Spouse/partner work access is more restricted than CSEP</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. IT Jobs in Ireland with Work Permit</h2>
      <p>Ireland is one of the most tech-dense economies in the world per capita. Dublin's "Silicon Docks" hosts European HQs for the world's largest technology companies. Most senior IT roles qualify for the Critical Skills Employment Permit.</p>

      <h3>Roles that commonly qualify for CSEP in IT</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Software Developer / Software Engineer</li>
        <li>Data Scientist / Data Analyst</li>
        <li>Cybersecurity Analyst / Security Engineer</li>
        <li>Cloud Architect / Solutions Architect</li>
        <li>DevOps Engineer / Platform Engineer</li>
        <li>Machine Learning Engineer / AI Researcher</li>
        <li>UX Designer (some classifications)</li>
        <li>IT Project Manager / Scrum Master</li>
        <li>Network Engineer</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Salary for IT roles in Ireland</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Junior Software Engineer:</strong> €45,000–€60,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Mid-level Engineer (3–5 years):</strong> €65,000–€90,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Senior Engineer:</strong> €90,000–€130,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Engineering Manager / Tech Lead:</strong> €110,000–€160,000+/year</li>
        <li><strong>Data Scientist:</strong> €65,000–€100,000/year</li>
        <li><strong>Note:</strong> Big Tech companies (Google, Meta, Apple) typically pay 20–40% above these market rates</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>3. Nursing Jobs in Ireland with Visa Sponsorship</h2>
      <p>Nurses are actively recruited internationally by the HSE (Health Service Executive) and private hospital groups in Ireland. Nursing is on the Critical Skills Eligible Roles list.</p>

      <h3>Requirements for international nurses</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>NMBI registration:</strong> Register with the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland (NMBI). Overseas applicants must have their qualifications assessed</li>
        <li><strong>English language:</strong> IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0) or equivalent</li>
        <li><strong>Application:</strong> <a href="https://www.nmbi.ie/Registration/Overseas-Qualified-Nurses" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">NMBI — Overseas Nurses</a></li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> 3–6 months for full registration</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>4. How the Permit Application Process Works</h2>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Receive a job offer</strong> from an Irish employer (or you can jointly apply — both employer and employee can initiate)</li>
        <li><strong>Determine the permit type:</strong> Critical Skills or General Employment Permit</li>
        <li><strong>Apply online</strong> at the <a href="https://dbei.gov.ie/en/What-We-Do/Workplace-and-Skills/Employment-Permits/Employment-Permit-Eligibility/Employment-Permit-Eligibility-Checker/" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">DETE Online Employment Permits System</a></li>
        <li><strong>Pay the application fee:</strong> €1,000 for Critical Skills (€500 if application is refused); €1,000 for General (€500 if refused)</li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> Currently 4–8 weeks (check live wait times on DETE website)</li>
        <li>Once the permit is issued, apply for an <strong>employment visa</strong> at your local Irish embassy (if you are not visa-exempt)</li>
        <li><strong>Arrive in Ireland</strong> and register with the Garda National Immigration Bureau (GNIB) within 90 days</li>
      </ol>

      <h2>5. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Does the Irish employer need a special licence to sponsor me?</h3>
      <p>No. Unlike the UK where employers need a Home Office sponsor licence, Irish employers can sponsor overseas workers without pre-registration. Any registered Irish company that meets basic compliance requirements can apply for an employment permit on behalf of a non-EU worker.</p>

      <h3>Can I change employers while on an Irish employment permit?</h3>
      <p>During the first year of a Critical Skills permit, you generally cannot change employer without a new permit. After the first year, transfers between employers in the same sector may be possible. After 2 years on a CSEP, you receive Stamp 4 which allows unrestricted work for any employer.</p>

      <h3>Is Ireland a viable route to EU citizenship?</h3>
      <p>Yes. After 5 years of continuous lawful residence in Ireland (including time on an employment permit), you can apply for Irish naturalisation. Irish citizenship grants EU citizenship, the right to live and work in any EU member state, and one of the world's most powerful passports.</p>
