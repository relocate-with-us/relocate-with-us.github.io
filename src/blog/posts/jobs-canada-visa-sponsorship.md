      <h2>1. Understanding Canadian Work Permits</h2>
      <p>Canadian work permits fall into two broad categories:</p>

      <h3>Open Work Permits</h3>
      <p>Allow you to work for <em>any</em> Canadian employer without employer-specific sponsorship. You can obtain an open work permit through:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP):</strong> After completing a Canadian study program of 8+ months</li>
        <li><strong>Spousal/partner of a skilled worker:</strong> If your partner holds a work or study permit in certain categories</li>
        <li><strong>International Experience Canada (IEC):</strong> Working holiday and youth exchange programs for citizens of eligible countries</li>
        <li><strong>Refugee claimants and protected persons:</strong> While awaiting a permanent decision</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Employer-Specific (Closed) Work Permits</h3>
      <p>Tie you to a specific employer. This is what most people mean by "visa sponsorship" in Canada. Obtained through:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA):</strong> Employer proves no Canadian could fill the role</li>
        <li><strong>LMIA-exempt work permits:</strong> Many categories are exempt (intra-company transfers, CUSMA/USMCA, significant benefit to Canada)</li>
        <li><strong>Global Talent Stream (GTS):</strong> Fast-tracked 2-week processing for tech and highly skilled workers</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. The LMIA Process: How Canadian Employers Sponsor Workers</h2>
      <p>The <strong>Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)</strong> is the main employer sponsorship mechanism. Before hiring a foreign worker, most Canadian employers must apply to Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) for an LMIA.</p>

      <h3>What an LMIA involves</h3>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Job advertisement:</strong> The employer advertises the position in Canada for a minimum of 4 weeks</li>
        <li><strong>LMIA application:</strong> Employer submits evidence they could not find a qualified Canadian to fill the role</li>
        <li><strong>Fee:</strong> CAD $1,000 per position (waived for some GTS categories)</li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> 2–5 months for standard LMIA; 2 weeks for Global Talent Stream</li>
        <li><strong>Positive LMIA:</strong> Once approved, you can apply for a work permit</li>
      </ol>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>LMIA-exempt categories:</strong> Many professional roles don't require an LMIA under the Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement (CUSMA), intra-company transfer provisions, or significant benefit to Canada provisions. If you are a citizen of the US, Mexico, or are being transferred within a multinational company, check whether you qualify for an LMIA-exempt permit before requesting LMIA sponsorship.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>3. Express Entry: Canada's Points-Based Permanent Residency System</h2>
      <p>Express Entry is not a work permit — it's Canada's primary pathway to <strong>permanent residency</strong>. Understanding it is essential because many Canadian employers sponsor workers with PR in mind.</p>

      <h3>How Express Entry works</h3>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Create an Express Entry profile</strong> at <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">IRCC Express Entry</a></li>
        <li>You are scored using the <strong>Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)</strong> based on age, education, language ability, work experience, and Canadian connections</li>
        <li>IRCC runs regular <strong>draws</strong>, inviting the highest-scoring candidates to apply for PR</li>
        <li>A <strong>job offer from a Canadian employer</strong> adds 50–200 CRS points, dramatically increasing your chances of being invited</li>
        <li>From invitation to PR: typically 6 months</li>
      </ol>

      <h3>Express Entry programs</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP):</strong> For overseas skilled workers with at least 1 year of skilled work experience</li>
        <li><strong>Canadian Experience Class (CEC):</strong> For people who already have 1+ year of Canadian work experience</li>
        <li><strong>Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP):</strong> For qualified tradespeople with a valid job offer or provincial certificate</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>4. Global Talent Stream (GTS): Fast-Track for Tech Workers</h2>
      <p>The <strong>Global Talent Stream</strong> under the Temporary Foreign Worker Program provides 2-week processing for highly skilled workers in eligible tech and STEM occupations. It's the fastest legal employment pathway in Canada.</p>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Category A:</strong> Referred by a designated partner (e.g., a Canadian tech industry association). No occupation list required</li>
        <li><strong>Category B:</strong> Employer needs a worker in a highly in-demand tech occupation from the <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/programs/foreign-workers/global-talent/requirements.html" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">GTS occupation list</a></li>
        <li>Both streams require the employer to commit to a <strong>Labour Market Benefits Plan</strong> — a commitment to hire or train additional Canadians</li>
        <li><strong>Eligible roles include:</strong> Software engineers, data scientists, cybersecurity analysts, cloud architects, AI/ML specialists</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)</h3>
      <p>Each Canadian province and territory runs its own immigration streams. Many PNPs have employer-driven streams where a provincial job offer leads directly to a nomination for permanent residency, bypassing or fast-tracking the federal Express Entry process.</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)</strong> — Employer Job Offer stream</li>
        <li><strong>British Columbia PNP</strong> — BC Tech stream for tech workers</li>
        <li><strong>Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP)</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)</strong> — International Skilled Worker category</li>
        <li><strong>Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program (MPNP)</strong> — Skilled Worker in Manitoba stream</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>7. How to Get a Canadian Work Visa Through an Employer: Step by Step</h2>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Find a Canadian employer willing to sponsor:</strong> Use the job boards above. Look for postings that mention "LMIA support", "work permit sponsorship", or "open to foreign workers"</li>
        <li><strong>Receive a job offer:</strong> The offer should be in writing and specify salary, position, and location</li>
        <li><strong>Employer applies for LMIA</strong> (or verifies LMIA-exempt status): This can take 2 weeks (GTS) to 5 months (standard)</li>
        <li><strong>LMIA approved → Apply for work permit:</strong> Submit your work permit application to IRCC with the LMIA number and job offer letter</li>
        <li><strong>Biometrics and processing:</strong> Attend a biometrics appointment at a VAC (Visa Application Centre) in your country</li>
        <li><strong>Work permit issued:</strong> Enter Canada and begin work</li>
        <li><strong>Build Express Entry profile</strong> with Canadian work experience to move toward PR</li>
      </ol>

      <h2>8. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can I get a job offer in Canada before I arrive?</h3>
      <p>Yes, and many people do. Apply from your home country, complete interviews remotely, and once the work permit is approved, fly to Canada. Some roles (particularly tech and healthcare) offer relocation assistance.</p>

      <h3>Do I need French to work in Canada?</h3>
      <p>Not for most roles outside Quebec. The majority of Canadian employers outside Quebec operate in English. However, French speakers receive additional CRS points in Express Entry, and Quebec has its own separate immigration system that strongly favours French proficiency.</p>

      <h3>How long does it take to get Canadian permanent residency through Express Entry?</h3>
      <p>With a strong CRS score (typically 470+ for a draw), you can receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) within months. From ITA to PR grant: approximately 6 months if your application is complete. Total timeline from first arriving in Canada as a worker to PR can be 2–3 years if you build Canadian experience to boost your CRS score.</p>
