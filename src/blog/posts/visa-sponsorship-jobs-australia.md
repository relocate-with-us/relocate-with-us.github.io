      <p>Australia is one of the most popular destinations for skilled workers — and its visa infrastructure reflects that. The <strong>Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa, subclass 482</strong>, is the main employer-sponsored pathway. This guide explains how it works, which occupations qualify, and how to find 482 sponsorship jobs in 2025.</p>

      <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 border-l-4 border-blue-400 dark:border-blue-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">

      <h2>1. How the 482 Visa Works</h2>
      <p>The 482 visa has three streams:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Short-Term Stream:</strong> Roles on the STSOL (Short-Term Skilled Occupation List). Up to 2 years. No direct PR pathway.</li>
        <li><strong>Medium-Term Stream:</strong> Roles on the MLTSSL (Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List). Up to 4 years. Leads to permanent residency after 3 years via the subclass 186 ENS visa.</li>
        <li><strong>Labour Agreement Stream:</strong> For employers with a formal government labour agreement (common in agriculture and mining).</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>What the employer must do</h3>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Be approved as a <strong>Standard Business Sponsor (SBS)</strong></li>
        <li>Nominate you for a position matching an eligible occupation</li>
        <li>Pay the <strong>Skilling Australians Fund (SAF) levy</strong> — AUD $1,200–$1,800/year</li>
        <li>Complete <strong>Labour Market Testing</strong> (prove they tried to hire locally first)</li>
        <li>Pay you at least the <strong>Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold (TSMIT)</strong> — currently AUD $70,000/year</li>
      </ol>

      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>2025 update:</strong> The TSMIT increased to AUD $70,000 in July 2023, up from $53,900. Always verify the current threshold on the <a href="https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/temporary-skill-shortage-482" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">official Home Affairs page</a>.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>2. The 482 Visa Occupation Lists</h2>
      <p>Your job title must appear on one of two lists:</p>

      <h3>Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) — 4-year visa + PR pathway</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Software and applications programmers</li>
        <li>Registered nurses (all specialisations)</li>
        <li>Civil, mechanical, and electrical engineers</li>
        <li>Accountants and auditors</li>
        <li>Medical practitioners and allied health professionals</li>
        <li>Secondary school teachers</li>
        <li>Construction project managers</li>
        <li>Farm and agricultural managers</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Short-Term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) — 2-year visa</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Cooks and chefs</li>
        <li>Hotel and hospitality managers</li>
        <li>Some technical and trade roles</li>
        <li>Web developers (some classifications)</li>
      </ul>

      <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 border-l-4 border-blue-400 dark:border-blue-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Check your occupation:</strong> Use the <a href="https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/working-in-australia/skill-occupation-list" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">official Skilled Occupation List tool</a> to see if your role qualifies and which stream applies.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>3. Industries That Sponsor the Most Visas</h2>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>🏥 Healthcare:</strong> Largest sponsoring sector — nurses, GPs, aged care, allied health</li>
        <li><strong>🏗️ Construction:</strong> Civil engineers, project managers, trades supervisors</li>
        <li><strong>🍽️ Hospitality:</strong> Chefs, cooks, hotel managers — particularly in tourism regions</li>
        <li><strong>💻 Technology:</strong> Software developers, cybersecurity analysts, ICT managers</li>
        <li><strong>🌾 Agriculture:</strong> Farm managers, agricultural scientists, horticulturalists</li>
        <li><strong>⚒️ Mining:</strong> Mining engineers, geologists, heavy plant operators</li>
      </ul>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">

        <li>Look for "we welcome international applications", "visa sponsorship available", or "relocation assistance provided"</li>

        <li>Work with a registered migration agent (RMA) to verify sponsor status before accepting an offer</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>5. AEWV Approved Employers List — Australia vs New Zealand</h2>
      <p>The <strong>Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)</strong> is New Zealand's employer-sponsored visa, not Australia's. Many people searching for "AEWV approved employers Australia" are looking for the equivalent — Australia's <strong>Standard Business Sponsor (SBS)</strong> register.</p>
      <p>Australia does not publish a public SBS employer list. Instead:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Ask any employer directly whether they hold current <strong>Standard Business Sponsor approval</strong></li>
        <li>Major hospital networks (Ramsay Health Care, St Vincent's), mining companies (BHP, Rio Tinto), and large hotel chains are almost always approved sponsors</li>
        <li>A registered migration agent can verify eligibility before you proceed</li>
      </ul>

See our <a href="/blog/posts/accredited-employer-work-visa-new-zealand/" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">New Zealand AEWV guide</a> for details.</p>

      <h2>6. Costs and Timeline</h2>
      <h3>For the applicant</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Visa application fee:</strong> AUD $3,115 (primary applicant, 2024–25)</li>
        <li><strong>Skills assessment:</strong> AUD $300–$900 (if required for your occupation)</li>
        <li><strong>English test:</strong> AUD $300–$400</li>
        <li><strong>Health examination:</strong> AUD $300–$500</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>For the employer</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Nomination fee:</strong> AUD $330</li>
        <li><strong>SAF levy:</strong> AUD $1,200–$1,800 per year of the visa</li>
        <li><strong>Total per hire:</strong> Typically AUD $5,000–$10,000+ including migration agent fees</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Processing time</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Nomination + visa combined:</strong> 75% processed within 77 days (2024 data)</li>
        <li><strong>Total timeline from offer:</strong> Budget 3–6 months including embassy steps</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>7. Pathway to Permanent Residency</h2>
      <p>If you are on the Medium-Term stream (MLTSSL occupation):</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>After <strong>3 years</strong> with your sponsoring employer → apply for <strong>Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS) visa, subclass 186</strong></li>
        <li>Alternatively, submit an Expression of Interest to SkillSelect for the <strong>Skilled Independent (189)</strong> or <strong>Skilled Nominated (190)</strong> visa</li>
        <li>Regional employers can sponsor permanent residency via the <strong>SESR visa, subclass 494</strong></li>
      </ul>

      <h2>8. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can my family join me in Australia on a 482 visa?</h3>
      <p>Yes. Your spouse/partner and dependent children can be secondary applicants. Your partner receives full work rights and can work for any Australian employer.</p>

      <h3>Can I change employers on a 482 visa?</h3>
      <p>Yes. Your new employer must be an approved SBS and lodge a new nomination. You cannot work for an unapproved employer. Once the nomination is approved, you can start with the new company.</p>

      <h3>Do I need a skills assessment?</h3>
      <p>It depends on your occupation. Engineers (Engineers Australia), nurses (AHPRA), and teachers (AITSL) typically require one. Check the requirements for your specific occupation code.</p>
