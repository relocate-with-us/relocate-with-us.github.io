      <p>Dublin hosts the European headquarters of Google, Meta, Stripe, and dozens of high-growth SaaS companies. For non-EEA software engineers, the <strong>Critical Skills Employment Permit</strong> is the main accelerated work route: it is designed for occupations where Ireland has persistent skill shortages, including ICT professionals. This guide explains how the permit works in practice, what salary levels matter, how long it takes, and how it differs from the standard General Employment Permit.</p>

      <h2>1. Critical Skills vs General Employment Permit</h2>
      <p>The <strong>Critical Skills Employment Permit</strong> targets highly skilled roles on Ireland's eligible occupation list, including many software engineering positions. Benefits typically include:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Faster labour market needs test:</strong> Employers are not required to advertise as extensively as for a General Permit for eligible roles.</li>
        <li><strong>Two-year route to Stamp 4:</strong> After holding a Critical Skills permit and meeting residence conditions, many holders can move toward longer-term residence (commonly described as a path toward Stamp 4 — confirm current rules with official Irish immigration guidance).</li>
        <li><strong>Spouse / partner work access:</strong> Eligible spouses and partners can often apply for a separate permit to work in Ireland — a major advantage over countries with restrictive partner rules.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>The <strong>General Employment Permit</strong> is broader but usually involves more employer compliance steps and may have different salary thresholds. Most large tech employers hiring senior engineers use Critical Skills when the role qualifies.</p>

      <h2>2. Salary thresholds and job titles</h2>
      <p>Immigration rules distinguish jobs by salary band and whether the occupation is on the Critical Skills list. In practice, software engineers in Dublin should assume:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Your <strong>remuneration must meet or exceed the minimum for your category</strong> as published by Ireland's Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment.</li>
        <li>Your <strong>job title and duties</strong> must align with an eligible ICT occupation — generic titles that do not match the role can cause permit issues.</li>
        <li>Employers almost always work with <strong>immigration counsel</strong> to classify the role correctly.</li>
      </ul>
      <div class="bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 border-l-4 border-yellow-400 dark:border-yellow-600 p-4 rounded-r-lg mb-6">
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 mb-0"><strong>Important:</strong> Salary floors and occupation lists change. Always verify the current figures on <a href="https://enterprise.gov.ie/" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">official Irish government sources</a> before you negotiate an offer.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>3. Timeline: from offer to first day in Dublin</h2>
      <p>Typical phases:</p>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Offer signed</strong> — employer gathers role description, salary, and company registration documents.</li>
        <li><strong>Permit application</strong> — submitted online; processing times vary by queue season.</li>
        <li><strong>Visa (if required)</strong> — non-visa-exempt nationalities apply for an entry visa to travel to Ireland.</li>
        <li><strong>Registration</strong> — after arrival, register with immigration and receive permission stamps.</li>
      </ol>
      <p>Many engineers budget <strong>8–14 weeks</strong> end-to-end, but embassy backlogs can extend this — start housing search only once you have realistic arrival dates.</p>

      <h2>4. Cost of living in Dublin (planning numbers)</h2>
      <p>Dublin is expensive relative to Berlin or Lisbon. Budget planning tips:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Rent:</strong> Expect the largest line item; many engineers flat-share at first.</li>
        <li><strong>Commute:</strong> Dart and buses are usable; some teams are hybrid.</li>
        <li><strong>Tax:</strong> Use our <a href="/tools/salary-calculator/" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline">salary calculator</a> to estimate net pay; Ireland uses progressive rates and USC/PRSI.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>5. Interview and negotiation tips</h2>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Ask whether HR has a <strong>dedicated immigration vendor</strong> — it signals experience with non-EEA hires.</li>
        <li>Confirm whether the offer exceeds the <strong>Critical Skills salary minimum</strong> for your role category.</li>
        <li>Negotiate <strong>relocation</strong> explicitly: flights, temporary housing, shipping, and spouse support if applicable.</li>
      </ul>
