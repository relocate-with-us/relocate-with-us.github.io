      <p>New Zealand replaced its previous Skilled Migrant visa and Essential Skills visa with the <strong>Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)</strong> in July 2022. The AEWV is now the main employer-sponsored work visa for overseas workers coming to New Zealand — and unlike Australia, New Zealand publishes a publicly searchable list of all accredited employers, making your job search significantly more targeted.</p>

      <h2>1. How the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) Works</h2>
      <p>The AEWV has three steps:</p>
      <ol class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Employer accreditation:</strong> The employer applies to Immigration New Zealand (INZ) to become an accredited employer. They must demonstrate they are a genuine business with good HR practices and that they cannot fill the role locally</li>
        <li><strong>Job check:</strong> For roles paying below NZD $29.66/hour (the median wage), the employer must complete a "job check" to confirm the role is genuine and they advertised locally first</li>
        <li><strong>Visa application:</strong> You apply for the AEWV using the employer's accreditation number and job token</li>
      </ol>

      <h3>Visa duration</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Up to <strong>3 years</strong> (or the length of the employment agreement, whichever is shorter)</li>
        <li>Can be extended once for a further 3 years (maximum 5 years in NZ on this visa before needing to spend time overseas or qualifying for a resident visa)</li>
        <li>Your employer can also be changed — you can apply for a variation to work for a new accredited employer without leaving New Zealand</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>2. The AEWV Accredited Employer List: How to Search It</h2>
      <p>New Zealand publishes a fully searchable public list of all AEWV-accredited employers. This is a significant advantage for job seekers — you can focus your applications exclusively on employers who can legally sponsor you immediately.</p>

      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Access the employer search tool at: <a href="https://employer-accreditation.immigration.govt.nz/search" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline font-medium" rel="noopener noreferrer">Immigration New Zealand — Search Accredited Employers</a></li>
        <li>Search by company name, industry, or region</li>
        <li>The list is updated regularly as new employers gain accreditation and existing ones lapse</li>
        <li>Employer accreditation has two levels: <strong>Standard</strong> (can hire up to 5 migrants) and <strong>High Volume</strong> (can hire unlimited migrants, with more obligations)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>3. Salary and Eligibility Requirements</h2>
      <p>The AEWV has a minimum wage requirement:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Minimum pay rate:</strong> NZD $29.66/hour (the median wage as of 2024). This is approximately NZD $61,692/year full-time</li>
        <li>Roles paying below this rate require a more rigorous job check process and are generally limited to roles on the Green List (see below)</li>
        <li><strong>Skilled worker roles above the median wage:</strong> Simpler process, faster processing</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>The Green List</h3>
      <p>New Zealand's <strong>Green List</strong> identifies occupations in high demand. Green List roles may qualify for:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>Straight to Residence:</strong> Some Green List Tier 1 occupations allow you to apply directly for permanent residency without needing to first hold a work visa</li>
        <li><strong>Work to Residence:</strong> Green List Tier 2 roles allow you to apply for permanent residency after 2 years on an AEWV</li>
        <li>The Green List includes roles in healthcare (nurses, doctors, GPs), engineering, construction, education, and aged care</li>
        <li>Check the current Green List at: <a href="https://www.immigration.govt.nz/new-zealand-visas/visas/visa/green-list-occupation-visa" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">Immigration NZ — Green List Occupations</a></li>
      </ul>

      <h2>4. Nursing Jobs in New Zealand with Visa Sponsorship</h2>
      <p>Nurses are on New Zealand's Green List Tier 1 — meaning registered nurses can apply <strong>straight to permanent residence</strong> without first holding a work visa. This makes New Zealand one of the most nurse-friendly destinations in the world for immigration.</p>

      <h3>Requirements for international nurses</h3>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li><strong>NCNZ registration:</strong> You must register with the <strong>Nursing Council of New Zealand (NCNZ)</strong>. The assessment process includes a qualification review, English language verification, and in many cases a period of supervised practice</li>
        <li><strong>English language:</strong> IELTS 7.0 overall (no band below 6.5), or OET B grade, or equivalent</li>
        <li><strong>Application:</strong> <a href="https://www.nursingcouncil.org.nz/overseas-nurses" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">NCNZ — Overseas Nurses</a></li>
        <li><strong>Processing time:</strong> 3–6 months for NCNZ registration</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>5. Care Worker Jobs in New Zealand 2025</h2>
      <p>Care workers (aged care, disability support) are in strong demand in New Zealand. Key facts:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>Care workers are on the Green List Tier 2 (Work to Residence pathway after 2 years)</li>
        <li>Many DHBs and private aged care providers hold High Volume AEWV accreditation and actively recruit overseas</li>
        <li>Wages for care workers have increased significantly following the 2022 pay equity settlement — most roles now pay NZD $25–$30/hour</li>
        <li>Popular source countries include Philippines, India, Fiji, and South Africa</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>6. Skilled Migrant Jobs in New Zealand</h2>
      <p>Beyond specific occupation lists, New Zealand also offers the <strong>Skilled Migrant Category Resident Visa</strong> for workers who meet points thresholds. Key 2025 information:</p>
      <ul class="space-y-2 mb-6">
        <li>The SMC was paused and revised in 2022. Under the reformed system, a job offer from a NZ employer and a salary above the median wage is the primary pathway</li>
        <li>For most applicants, the AEWV → Green List Work to Residence pathway is now more straightforward than the points-based SMC</li>
        <li>High CRS scorers in technology, healthcare, and engineering have the strongest pathways to permanent residency</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>8. Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
      <h3>Can I bring my family to New Zealand on an AEWV?</h3>
      <p>Yes. Your partner and dependent children can apply for visitor visas or, in many cases, partner work visas that allow them to work for any employer in New Zealand.</p>

      <h3>How do I verify if an employer is currently accredited?</h3>
      <p>Use the <a href="https://employer-accreditation.immigration.govt.nz/search" target="_blank" class="text-brand-600 dark:text-brand-400 hover:underline" rel="noopener noreferrer">Immigration NZ accredited employer search tool</a>. Accreditation must be current at the time you apply for your visa — an employer whose accreditation has lapsed cannot sponsor you.</p>

      <h3>What happens to my visa if I change employers?</h3>
      <p>You can transfer to a new accredited employer by applying for a variation of your AEWV. You can also work for a new employer for up to 3 months while the variation is being processed, as long as the new employer is accredited.</p>
