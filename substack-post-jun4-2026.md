## 💡 This Week's Relocation Tip

**The circular problem in Amsterdam — and how to solve it:**

When you arrive in Amsterdam, you'll quickly discover: you need a BSN to open a bank account, you need a bank account for landlords to accept you, and you need an address to get your BSN. It's a classic Catch-22.

Here's the order that actually works:

1. Use your **company's temporary accommodation** (or book an Airbnb for the first few weeks)
2. Register at the **gemeente (Amsterdam municipality)** using that address → you get your BSN on the spot
3. Use the BSN to open a **Bunq account** (fully online, no branch visit needed, works for expats without a Dutch address yet)
4. Use the bank account + BSN to search for permanent housing seriously

The whole thing takes about 2 weeks if you're organized. Don't rush into a permanent flat before you have your BSN — you'll lose every offer.
