# Foreword: Why this book exists

I run [VisaJobs.xyz](https://visajobs.xyz). Every week I get the same emails:

> "How do I find companies that actually sponsor visas?"
> "My resume gets ignored — is it because I'm a foreigner?"
> "Recruiters never reply to my LinkedIn messages. What am I doing wrong?"
> "I got an interview but I don't know how to bring up the visa thing."
> "I have an offer — am I being underpaid because they know I need sponsorship?"

I wrote answers, made blog posts, built a job board, started a Telegram, posted on LinkedIn. None of it was enough, because the problem isn't a single missing piece of information. It's that nobody assembles the whole system in one place.

This book is that system.

It's the one I built for myself, refined by helping hundreds of people land visa-sponsored roles, and updated to 2026 with the salary thresholds, sponsor registers, and platform changes that actually matter right now.

## Who this is for

**You should read this if:**

- You're a software engineer, data scientist, ML/AI engineer, DevOps/SRE, designer, or PM
- You have at least 1 year of professional experience (or a strong CS degree from a recognized university)
- You want to work in another country and need a visa sponsored
- You're tired of generic "how to write a CV" advice that doesn't account for being international

**You should probably not read this if:**

- You have zero technical or professional skills yet — start with building those first
- You're looking for a way to get a visa without a job offer (with rare exceptions, this isn't a thing for tech)
- You expect a magic shortcut. There isn't one. There's just a much, much better system than what most people are doing

## The promise

If you do the work in this book — and I mean *do* it, not just read it — you will:

1. Apply to the right jobs at the right companies (not waste 200 applications on companies that don't sponsor)
2. Have a resume that beats both ATS filters and actual humans
3. Have a repeatable workflow to tailor your resume in 5 minutes per application
4. Know exactly what to say to recruiters, hiring managers, and referrers
5. Walk into interviews knowing the cultural and visa-related questions before they ask them
6. Negotiate confidently because you'll know what you're worth in *that* country, not yours
7. Move countries with a checklist, not panic

That's the system. Let's start.
