# Part 2 — Where the jobs actually live

Most candidates apply where it's easiest to apply: LinkedIn, Indeed, the front page of Google. That's also where 200 other people are applying to the same job. You need a wider funnel.

Here's the full map of where visa-sponsored tech jobs actually get posted in 2026, ranked by signal-to-noise for international candidates.

## Chapter 3. The job boards that work

### Tier 1 — Visa-first, signal-dense

These boards exist specifically to surface jobs with relocation/visa sponsorship. Use these first, every search session.

**[VisaJobs.xyz](https://visajobs.xyz)** — disclosure, this is mine. Every listing is verified for sponsorship. Free. Filter by country, role, remote.

**[Relocate.me](https://relocate.me)** — niche tech relocation board, ~1M+ users, 100+ countries covered. Strong for senior roles, payments-heavy in the EU.

**[Jaabz](https://jaabz.com)** — visa-sponsorship and relocation jobs, well-organised by country. Their Germany list is solid.

**[The Global Move](https://relocate.me/the-global-move)** — Relocate.me's curated weekly newsletter, ~100 hand-picked sponsorship roles per issue. The free tier alone is worth subscribing to.

**[Honeypot](https://www.honeypot.io)** — companies apply to *you*, not the other way around. Strongest in DACH and the Netherlands. Set up a profile once, get inbound, vet sponsorship in the first reply.

**[Relocateviawork](    )** — newer subscription board ($19/mo), curated visa-sponsored roles globally. Worth a one-month trial during an active search.

**[ArbeitNow](https://arbeitnow.com)** — German jobs with English working language, often sponsorship-friendly.

### Tier 3 — General boards (use with strong filters)

These have everything but you need to filter aggressively or you'll drown.

**LinkedIn Jobs** — still the single largest source. Use these search operators:

```
("visa sponsorship" OR "relocation" OR "Blue Card" OR "Skilled Worker" OR "H-1B") AND (your role)
```

Combine with location filter set to your target country. Save the search and turn on alerts.

![LinkedIn jobs — using sponsorship search operators](../screenshots/ss-10-linkedin-search-operators.png)
*LinkedIn has no native "visa sponsorship" filter. These operators are the workaround. Save the search and tick "Alert me" — you'll get an email every time a new matching job posts.*

**Wellfound, Otta, Welcome to the Jungle** — startup-heavy, modern filters, often a "willing to sponsor" tag.

**Built In** ([builtin.com](https://builtin.com)) — strong for US startup ecosystem (NYC, SF, Boston, Austin). Has remote and relocation filters.

**YC Work at a Startup** ([workatastartup.com](https://workatastartup.com)) — Y Combinator companies. Many will sponsor for the right hire.

**Hacker News "Who is Hiring"** — first of every month, [news.ycombinator.com](https://news.ycombinator.com). Search the thread for "visa," "sponsor," "relocate," "REMOTE."

**Stack Overflow Jobs** — discontinued in early 2024, but its replacement [Stack Overflow Talent](https://stackoverflowsolutions.com/talent) and the new [The Practical Engineer Job Board](https://jobs.pragmaticengineer.com) (Gergely Orosz's curated board) are excellent for senior roles with sponsorship.

### Tier 4 — Direct careers pages (the highest-converting source)

This is the biggest unlock most candidates miss: **applying directly through a company's careers page converts 2–4× better than LinkedIn**, because:

1. The recruiter sees you as proactive, not lazy
2. Some ATSs treat direct applicants higher in the queue
3. You skip the LinkedIn "easy apply" pile that has 800 applicants

Once you have your shortlist of sponsor companies (Chapter 4), bookmark their careers pages and check them weekly. Most companies post jobs on their site 1–7 days *before* they reach LinkedIn.

### How to actually use job boards (the routine)

Every Monday, 60 minutes:

1. Open your saved searches on Tier 1 + Tier 2 boards (10 mins)
2. Skim the Practical Engineer board and HN "Who is Hiring" if it's first of month (10 mins)
3. Check the careers pages of your top 20 target companies (20 mins)
4. Add every plausible role to a tracking spreadsheet (link in Appendix D) (10 mins)
5. Pick the 5–8 most aligned. These are this week's applications. (10 mins)

Friday, 4 hours:

1. Run the AI tailoring loop (Part 3) on each of the 5–8
2. Submit
3. Send LinkedIn messages to recruiters / employees at those companies (Part 4)
4. Update tracker

That's the rhythm. Boring, repeatable, and effective.

### What about agencies and headhunters?

For senior+ roles, yes — but selectively. Specific names worth contacting:

- **Storm4** — climate-tech focus, EU + US
- **Robert Half** / **Hays Technology** — large agencies, useful when they have an exclusive
- **Stoneseed**, **Sthree** — UK-strong
- **Riviera Partners**, **True Search** — US executive tech search
- **Tobu Recruitment**, **Spotted Zebra**, **Cundall Manufacturing** — niche EU plays

Don't sign with multiple agencies for the same role — they'll fight over commission and you'll get blocked from applying directly.

## Chapter 4. The sponsor lists

This is the chapter most people will paste into their bookmarks and refer back to monthly. The lists are organised by country, with the visa pathway, salary threshold, and a starter list of confirmed sponsors. Use the bonus spreadsheet for the full version.

### How to verify a company sponsors (do this before every application)

A 60-second verification routine:

1. **Search the official register** if one exists (UK, NL — see below)
2. **Check the company's careers page** for "visa sponsorship," "relocation," or "international applicants" language
3. **Search LinkedIn for "visa" or "relocation" within the company** (e.g., go to the People tab and search; employees often mention their visa journey)
4. **Search Glassdoor + Blind** for the company name + "visa" / "sponsorship"
5. **Search the H-1B databases** (US) or government registers (UK, NL)

If 2 of these 5 give you green flags, apply.

### 4.1 — United States (H-1B + alternatives)

The US is the highest-paying market and the most painful visa-wise. The primary path for tech is H-1B, which has an annual lottery (cap of 85,000 for fiscal year 2026, ~3× oversubscribed). Filing window is March, results in late March/April, work starts October 1.

**Salary thresholds:** Set by DOL prevailing wage; for senior tech in tier-1 metros (SF, NYC, Seattle), expect $130k–$300k+ depending on level.

**Alternative US visa paths** (don't sleep on these):

- **Cap-exempt H-1B** — universities, non-profits, research institutions don't go through the lottery.

### 4.2 — United Kingdom (Skilled Worker visa)

The UK has the best public infrastructure for verifying sponsors of any major country.

**Salary threshold (2026):** £38,700 minimum, or "going rate" for the SOC code, whichever is higher. Many tech roles have higher going rates.

**Verification:** Download the [official Register of Licensed Sponsors](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-workers). Updated monthly. CSV file ~12MB. **Only A-rated sponsors can issue new Certificates of Sponsorship.** B-rated cannot — skip them.

Easier interfaces over the same data:
- [HuntUKVisaSponsors.com](https://huntukvisasponsors.com) — searchable
- [SponsorList.co.uk](https://sponsorlist.co.uk) — searchable + role categorisation

**Top UK tech sponsors (by visas issued):**

- TCS (13,500+), Cognizant (4,200+), Infosys (3,100+), EY (2,900+), Deloitte, KPMG, PwC, Accenture
- **Big tech UK offices:** Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Salesforce, Oracle
- **Fintech:** Revolut, Monzo, Wise, Starling, GoCardless, Curve, Bondaval, Plaid
- **Tech:** Cloudflare, Snyk, Stripe (London), DeepMind, Mistral AI (London office), Robin AI, Improbable, Hopin (post-pivot remnants)
- **Consumer:** Spotify (London), Deliveroo, Just Eat, Bumble
- **Trading / quant:** Jane Street, Citadel, G-Research, Optiver (London desk), DRW, Hudson River Trading

**Path:** Sponsor licence + Certificate of Sponsorship → 3-week visa decision → settle (ILR) after 5 years.

### 4.3 — Germany (EU Blue Card + IT Specialist visa)

Germany is the largest open market for tech in Europe. Most companies don't need any "sponsor licence" — they just hire you and apply for the Blue Card on your behalf.

**Salary thresholds (2026):**
- **EU Blue Card (IT shortage occupations):** €45,934.20/year
- **EU Blue Card (general):** €50,700/year approximately
- **IT Specialist Visa (no degree, 3+ years experience):** ~€51,000/year
- **PR (Niederlassungserlaubnis):** 21 months on Blue Card with B1 German, or 27 months without

**Top sponsors (alphabetical, all confirmed Blue Card sponsors):**

- **Berlin:** Zalando, N26, Delivery Hero, HelloFresh, SoundCloud, Contentful, Miro, Trade Republic, Wayfair, Babbel, GetYourGuide, Tier Mobility, Solarisbank, Raisin, Pitch, Personio (also Munich), Ada Health, Helsing, Mistral AI office, Hopin remnants
- **Munich:** Personio, NavVis, Celonis, Stylight, Konux, Konvert, BMW (software), Siemens, Allianz X, Microsoft Munich, Salesforce, Apple
- **Hamburg:** ABOUT YOU, awork, Adjust (Bytedance), XING (Newwork SE), Otto Group tech, Statista, Holidu (also Munich)
- **Walldorf:** SAP (the largest single tech employer in Germany)
- **Cologne / Düsseldorf:** Trivago, smava, Xing
- **Stuttgart:** Bosch, Daimler/Mercedes-Benz, Porsche Digital
- **Karlsruhe:** 1&1, IONOS, EnBW

**Verification:** No public register. Use Make-it-in-Germany's job listings, the company's careers page, and LinkedIn People search.

### 4.4 — Netherlands (Highly Skilled Migrant + EU Blue Card)

The Netherlands has the *fastest* legal route in Europe. A "Recognised Sponsor" can hire you and have you in the country within 4–8 weeks.

**Salary thresholds (2026, monthly gross excl. holiday allowance):**
- **HSM age 30+:** €5,942/month (~€71,300/year before 13th-month / holiday)
- **HSM under 30:** €4,357/month (~€52,300/year)
- **Orientation Year graduates:** €3,122/month
- **EU Blue Card:** €5,942/month

**The 30% ruling** (huge): up to 30% of gross salary tax-free for the first ~5 years (transitioning to 27% from 2027 for new arrivals). On a €100k salary, that's €25–30k tax-free annually. Cap at €78,600 of tax-free allowance for 2026. Get this in your contract before you sign.

**Verification:** [IND Public Register of Recognised Sponsors](https://ind.nl/en/public-register-recognised-sponsors). If a company is on this list, they can hire you next week.

![IND Netherlands — public register of recognised sponsors](../screenshots/ss-04-ind-nl-public-register.png)
*Search any company name. If they appear here with status "Recognized sponsor", they can file your permit online and have a decision in 2 weeks. If they're not listed, they can't sponsor you — no exceptions.*

**Top sponsors:**

- **Amsterdam:** Booking.com, Adyen, Mollie, Optiver (highest tech comp in EU), IMC, Flow Traders, Catawiki, MessageBird/Bird, Databricks, Uber EU, Picnic, Backbase, Bunq, Bitvavo
- **Rotterdam:** Coolblue, Swisscom NL, Mendix
- **Eindhoven:** ASML (one of the most valuable companies in Europe — chip lithography), Philips, NXP
- **Utrecht / The Hague:** Salesforce, Cisco

### 4.5 — Switzerland (B-permit, EU/EFTA vs third-country)

Switzerland is a compensation outlier — staff engineers at Google Zurich earn $400k+ TC. But quotas exist for non-EU/EFTA citizens (~8,500 B-permits/year split across cantons).

**Salary thresholds:** No statutory minimum, but you must be paid "according to industry and local norms" — practically, this is CHF 90k+ for any tech role, and CHF 130k–250k+ for senior IC at top employers.

**Top sponsors:**
- **Zurich:** Google, Meta, Microsoft, Disney Streaming, Cisco, IBM Research, Apple, Magic Leap remnants, Squirro, Beekeeper, Scandit
- **Geneva / Lausanne:** Logitech, Nestlé, Procter & Gamble (digital), CERN (research roles)
- **Basel:** Roche, Novartis (data/ML roles)
- **Banks (Zurich):** UBS, Credit Suisse (now part of UBS), Julius Baer, Sygnum, Bitcoin Suisse
- **Trading:** IMC (Zug), DRW, Jump Trading

**Pathways:**
- **B-permit** — 1-year renewable, can transition to C-permit (PR) after 10 years (5 if from a privileged country)
- **L-permit** — short-term 1-year, less flexibility
- **EU/EFTA** — easier; no quota

**Verification:** Cantonal — Zurich is the easiest, Geneva second. Each canton publishes its own quota and approval data.

### 4.6 — Bonus countries: where to apply when the Big 5 are crowded

Don't ignore these — for many candidates, they're actually the best fit.

**Ireland (Critical Skills Employment Permit):**
- Salary threshold: €38,000+ for ICT roles, €64,000+ for the unrestricted CSEP
- Top sponsors: Stripe (Dublin EU HQ), Google, Meta, Apple, Microsoft, Salesforce, Workday, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Intercom, Workhuman, Infineon (Cork), HubSpot
- Path to PR: 5 years
- English-first, fastest decision turnaround in EU after NL

**Sweden (Work permit):**
- Salary threshold: SEK 28,480/month (~€2,500), but tech salaries usually 2–3× this
- Top sponsors: Spotify (Stockholm HQ), Klarna, King (Activision), Embark Studios, iZettle, Truecaller, Voi, Tink
- Path to PR: 4 years (faster than most of EU)

**Denmark (Pay Limit Scheme + Fast-Track):**
- Salary threshold: DKK 514,000/year (~€69,000)
- Top sponsors: Pleo, Trustpilot, Unity (Copenhagen), Zendesk, Microsoft, Maersk, GN
- Fast-Track Scheme: 10-day processing for "Certified Companies"

**Estonia (D-visa + work permit):**
- Salary threshold: ~€1,800/month (1.24× national average)
- Top sponsors: Bolt (Tallinn HQ), Wise, Wolt (post-acquisition), Veriff, Pipedrive, Skype remnants, Twilio
- Digital-first immigration system, 30-day processing

- **GitLab** (all-remote, 60+ countries)
- **Automattic** (WordPress.com, 90+ countries)
- **Doist** (Todoist)
- **Buffer**

- **Zapier**
