## Appendix C — Glossary

Terms that actually need explaining — most are used in the visa chapters.

**Anabin** — German database of recognised foreign degree equivalencies. Check yours before applying. ([anabin.kmk.org](https://anabin.kmk.org))

**Anmeldung** — German address registration. Required within 14 days of moving in. Without it you can't open a bank account or get a residence permit.

**Apostille** — Hague Convention certification that makes a document issued in one country legally valid in another. Get these done at home before you leave — retroactively is painful.

**Arbeitszeugnis** — German employment reference letter. Uses coded language; Germans take it seriously. If you don't have one from a German employer, prepare a brief explanation.

**ATS** — Applicant Tracking System. Software that filters resumes before a human sees them. Up to 75% of applications never reach a person. This is why the keyword pass (Prompt 3) exists.

**B/C-permit** — Swiss residence permits. B = renewable annual permit. C = permanent residency, issued after 5–10 years.

**Beckham Law** — Spain's flat-tax regime for highly qualified new residents: 24% flat rate for 6 years. Comparable to the Dutch 30% ruling.

**BSN** — Dutch national identity / tax number. You need it to open a bank account, register with a GP, and file taxes.

**CEFR** — Language proficiency scale: A1 (beginner) → C2 (native-equivalent). Use it on your resume: "German B2, English C2."

**CoS** — Certificate of Sponsorship (UK). Your employer issues this; it's a unique code you use in your visa application.

**CRS** — Comprehensive Ranking System (Canada Express Entry). A points score that determines whether you get invited to apply for PR.

**CSEP** — Critical Skills Employment Permit (Ireland). Covers most tech roles; fast track to PR.

**EOR** — Employer of Record. A company like Deel or Remote.com that legally employs you in your country on behalf of a foreign company. Useful for remote-first roles that don't require a visa.

**EU Blue Card** — EU-wide residence permit for highly qualified workers. Different salary thresholds per country. Germany's is the most attractive in 2026.

**FBAR / FATCA** — US foreign asset reporting requirements. If you're a US citizen living abroad with foreign bank accounts > $10k, you must file. Penalties are severe; get a CPA.

**Going rate** — UK Skilled Worker visa term. The minimum salary for your specific job's SOC code, set by the Home Office. Sometimes higher than the £38,700 floor.

**IHS** — Immigration Health Surcharge (UK). £1,035/year of visa duration — charged upfront. Push your employer to cover this; reputable companies do.

**ILR** — Indefinite Leave to Remain. UK permanent residency. Gateway to British citizenship (1 more year).

**Kennismigrant** — Dutch "Highly Skilled Migrant" permit. Fastest legal employment route in Europe — 2-week decisions when your employer is a Recognised Sponsor.

**LCA** — Labor Condition Application (US H-1B). The employer files this with the DOL before petitioning USCIS. LCA data is public — this is what MyVisaJobs shows.

**Lebenslauf** — German CV format. Often includes professional photo, DOB, and signed at the bottom. Required at traditional companies; optional at startups.

**PERM / I-140** — US employer-sponsored green card stages. PERM = labour certification. I-140 = immigration petition. Priority date set at PERM filing; for some nationalities the wait is decades.

**30% ruling (NL)** — Up to 30% of gross salary tax-free for expats in the Netherlands, for approximately 5 years. Transitioning to 27% from 2027 for new arrivals. Cap: €78,600 tax-free per year in 2026. Get it in your contract before signing.

**Recognised Sponsor (NL)** — An IND-approved company authorised to file Kennismigrant permits. Check the [IND public register](https://ind.nl/en/public-register-recognised-sponsors) before applying. If they're not listed, they cannot sponsor you.

**Schufa** — German credit/identity check. Almost all German landlords require it before signing a lease. Request yours early (free once/year at schufa.de).
