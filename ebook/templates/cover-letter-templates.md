# Cover Letter Templates — by country

In some countries cover letters are mandatory; in others they're a waste of time. This file covers each country's expectations with a ready-to-edit template.

**Universal rules:**
- One page, never two
- Open with something about *them*, not yourself
- Cite one specific, quantified achievement that maps to the JD
- Address the visa/relocation question explicitly
- Don't open with "I am writing to apply for..." — it screams generic

---

## Germany — formal *Anschreiben* (mandatory for traditional companies, optional for startups)

```
[Your full name]
[Your address]
[Email] · [Phone]
[Date]

[Hiring manager full name]
[Title]
[Company]
[Company address]

Re: Bewerbung als [Role title] (Referenz [job ID if available])

Sehr geehrte/r Frau/Herr [Surname],

[Opening: 1 sentence about the company specifically. Reference a recent
product launch, paper, or strategic announcement. Show you've read more
than the careers page.]

Mit [N years] Erfahrung als [your current role] bei [your current
company] bringe ich genau die Kombination aus [skill A] und [skill B]
mit, die in Ihrer Stellenanzeige als zentral beschrieben wird.
Insbesondere [specific quantified achievement that maps to the JD].

Mein Hintergrund umfasst [transferable angle — something from another
domain or stack that's non-obvious value for this role]. Ich bin daran
interessiert, diese Erfahrung in [specific area of the company's work]
einzubringen.

Ich verfüge über die Voraussetzungen für die EU Blue Card aufgrund
[your degree / experience]. Ich kann innerhalb von [N] Wochen nach
einer Vertragsunterzeichnung nach [city] umziehen. Die Unterlagen für
die Anerkennung meines Abschlusses (anabin/ZAB) liegen bereits vor.

Über die Gelegenheit zu einem persönlichen Gespräch würde ich mich
sehr freuen.

Mit freundlichen Grüßen,

[Signature — yes, sign with a real signature, scan, embed]
[Typed name]
```

**English version (modern startups):**

```
Dear [Name],

[Specific sentence about a recent thing the company shipped or is
known for, showing you've actually paid attention.] That's the kind
of work I want to be doing next.

I've spent the last [N] years as a [your role] at [your company],
focused on [domain]. The most relevant evidence for your role:
[one quantified achievement, with metric and tools].

I bring a non-obvious angle from [other domain / stack / experience]
— specifically [brief transferable value statement].

I'm currently based in [country] and qualify for the EU Blue Card
based on my [degree] and the role's salary level. I can relocate
to [city] within [N] weeks of an offer.

Glad to talk further — let me know what works.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## Netherlands — short motivation letter

Dutch employers want short, direct, no-nonsense letters. 4 paragraphs max, half-page is fine.

```
Dear [Name],

[One sentence about the company that proves you read more than the
careers page. Direct, no flattery.]

Most relevant background: [one quantified achievement that maps to
the JD, including tools, scope, and outcome].

[One paragraph on transferable angle / why you specifically.]

I'm currently based in [country]. I qualify as a Highly Skilled
Migrant under the IND criteria (salary > €5,942/month for 30+, or
€4,357 for under 30) and the 30% ruling. Available within [N] weeks.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## United Kingdom — concise cover letter

Expected for most roles, but can be brief. Focus on fit, not life story.

```
Dear [Name],

[One sentence on the company — specific, recent, factual.]

[One paragraph: the strongest single piece of evidence I'm a fit.
Quantified.]

[One paragraph: the transferable angle / why me specifically over
a domestic candidate.]

I'm based in [country] and would require Skilled Worker visa
sponsorship. I'm aware of the £38,700 minimum salary threshold and
the going rate for [SOC code if you know it]. I can relocate to
[city] within [N] weeks of CoS.

Looking forward to talking.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## Switzerland — formal cover letter (mandatory)

Swiss applications are formal. Address by surname, formal sign-off.

```
Dear Mr/Ms [Surname],

[One specific sentence about the company — Swiss employers respect
research. Reference their place in the industry, a recent product,
or a strategic move.]

I have spent [N] years as a [role] at [company], where I [one
quantified achievement directly relevant to the role].

[One paragraph on transferable angle / specific Swiss-relevant
experience: cross-cultural teams, multilingual ability, etc.]

I am currently based in [country] and would require a B-permit.
Based on my [degree / experience] and the salary range typical for
this role in [canton], I expect to qualify. I can relocate to
[city] within [N] weeks of contract signing.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss the role further.

Yours sincerely,

[Signature]
[Typed name]
```

## United States — minimal, often optional

Most US tech companies don't require cover letters. When they do, keep it short.

```
Dear [Hiring Manager],

[One sentence on the company. Specific.]

I'm a [years]-year [role] currently at [company]. The most relevant
evidence for [Role at Company]: [one quantified achievement].

I'd bring [one specific transferable angle / unique experience] to
the team.

I'm currently authorized to work in [country] and would require
H-1B sponsorship for a US role. I'm aware of the timeline (~6-9
months from selection to October 1 start) and have all required
documentation ready (degree, transcripts, prior employment letters).

Available to talk anytime.

Best,
[Your name]
```

[Date]

[Signature]

## Spain / Portugal — bilingual approach

Spanish and Portuguese employers in tech often accept English. Submit both if you can write the local one well.

**English:**

```
Dear [Name],

[One sentence on the company — specific, recent.]

Most relevant background for [Role]: [one quantified achievement
mapped to the JD].

I bring a non-obvious angle: [transferable value statement].

I'm currently based in [country] and qualify for the Highly
Qualified Professional visa (Spain) / Tech Visa (Portugal) based
on my [degree / experience] and the offered salary level. I can
relocate to [city] within [N] weeks.

I'd welcome the chance to discuss this further.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Spanish version:**

```
Estimado/a [Nombre],

[Una frase específica sobre la empresa — proyecto reciente, paper,
posicionamiento.]

Cuento con [N años] como [tu rol] en [tu empresa]. Lo más
relevante para [Role]: [logro cuantificado que mapea al JD].

[Un párrafo sobre el ángulo transferible.]

Estoy basado/a en [país] y cumplo los requisitos para el visado de
Profesional Altamente Cualificado en base a [titulación /
experiencia] y el salario del puesto. Puedo trasladarme a
[ciudad] en [N] semanas tras la firma del contrato.

Quedo a su disposición para una entrevista.

Saludos cordiales,
[Nombre]
```

## Sweden / Denmark / Norway — *personligt brev*

Scandinavian cover letters value humility and clarity. Don't oversell.

```
Hi [Name],

[One sentence about the company, factual.]

I've spent [N] years as a [role]. Recent relevant work:
[one quantified achievement, no inflation].

Why this team specifically: [one specific reason — a paper, a
design philosophy, a domain].

I'm currently based in [country]. Available [start date]. The
work permit application is straightforward given [salary level /
fast-track scheme — be specific to country].

Happy to talk if useful.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Ireland — friendly but professional

Tech multinationals (Stripe, Google, Meta Dublin) accept US-style minimal cover letters. Local Irish companies prefer slightly more conversational ones.

```
Hi [Name],

[Specific sentence on the company — recent product, recent press,
known market position.]

Currently a [role] at [company], focused on [domain]. Most
relevant achievement for [Role at Company]: [quantified result].

I'd bring [transferable angle].

I'm based in [country] and qualify for the Critical Skills
Employment Permit based on my [degree/experience] and the role's
salary (above €38,000 ICT threshold). I can relocate to [city]
within [N] weeks.

Looking forward to it.

Cheers,
[Your name]
```

---

## A note on tone matching

Skim the company's website, blog, and LinkedIn to figure out their voice. A bank reads "best regards"; a startup reads "cheers." Match.

When in doubt, slightly more formal beats slightly less formal. You can always relax later. You can't easily un-do "Dude, your stuff is sick."
