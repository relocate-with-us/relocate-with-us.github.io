## Why $6 is too low

Your $6 instinct is the "impulse-buy" sweet spot, and it's not wrong — it just doesn't match what you've actually built.

What's in this product:
- A 70+ page playbook covering 7 countries with current 2026 visa data
- A LaTeX resume template that takes ~90 minutes to set up cleanly from scratch
- A 5-prompt AI workflow that has real, measurable time savings (60 min → 5 min per application)
- 11 outreach templates with reply-rate data
- 7 country-specific cover letter templates
- A 90-row sponsor spreadsheet starter
- Free updates for life

Comparable products in the market:
- **CSCareers tier productized resume reviews:** $50–$150 per review
- **Generic "How to write a CV" PDFs:** $9–$19, much less content
- **Practical Engineer Job Search Guide (Gergely Orosz):** $39, smaller scope
- **TopCV professional rewrite:** $200+
- **Country-specific relocation guides on Gumroad:** $15–$30 typically

At $6 you're underpricing by 3–5×. People who would pay $6 will also pay $19 — the buying decision is the same threshold. People who *shouldn't* pay $6 won't pay $19 either, and frankly aren't your customer.

### Recommended pricing

**Single-tier:**
- Regular price: **$19**
- Launch sale: **$9 for the first 72 hours**, with a clear countdown
- Black Friday / annual sale: $14

**OR a multi-tier (slightly more revenue, more setup work):**
- **Tier 1 — Ebook only:** $14
- **Tier 2 — Ebook + LaTeX template + AI prompts + outreach + cover letters:** $24
- **Tier 3 — Tier 2 + 30-min 1:1 consult with you:** $79–$149

Multi-tier is usually 30–50% more revenue per visitor. Downside: more support load and inventory of consult slots.

For a clean v1 launch, go single-tier at **$19** with the **$9 launch sale**. Add tiers in v2.

## The 7-day launch plan

Tuned to your specific channels: 6k Mailchimp, 11k LinkedIn, 7k Telegram, 4k Twitter, 2k/mo website visitors.

### T-7 days — pre-launch teaser

**Goal:** seed curiosity. No selling yet.

- **Twitter:** post a single thread on "the 5 most underused job boards for visa-sponsored tech roles" — pull material from Chapter 3. End with: *"I'm putting together the full system in a single ebook. Drop a 🇪🇺 if you'd want it."*
- **LinkedIn:** a long-form post with 3 specific tactics from the book (e.g., the LinkedIn search operators for visa-sponsored jobs, the recruiter ping template). End with: *"This is a fraction of the system I've documented. Anyone interested in a full version, comment below."*
- **Telegram:** 1 message with a screenshot of the table of contents. *"Working on this. Worth $19? Reply yes/no/maybe."*

This is your **demand validation**. Look at the engagement. If <50 strong signals, postpone and refine. If 200+, you have a launch.

### T-3 days — final teaser + waitlist

- **Email (Mailchimp):** announce the launch date with a 24-hour exclusive discount for subscribers ($9 instead of $19). Make them *click to claim* — that gives you a "warm" segment.
- **Twitter / LinkedIn:** "Launching Tuesday. Early-bird discount for the first 72 hours. Reply to get notified."
- **Telegram:** pinned message with launch countdown.
- **Website (visajobs.xyz):** discreet header bar — *"Coming Tuesday: The International Tech Job Playbook. [Click for early access]"*

### T-0 — launch day

A single coordinated 4-hour push.

**Hour 0 (pick a Tuesday at 14:00 UTC — best for catching both EU evening and US morning):**
- Email blast to entire Mailchimp list with the buy link + $9 launch code
- Twitter post: *"It's live. The International Tech Job Playbook — 70+ pages, LaTeX template, AI workflow, outreach scripts, all bundled. $9 for the next 72 hours, $19 after."* + screenshots of 2-3 best pages
- LinkedIn post: longer-form, lead with one strong tactic from the book, ending with the link
- Telegram: short message + buy link, pinned
- Website: temporary banner CTA at the top of every page

**Hour 1-2:** reply to every comment / DM personally. This is where 10-20% of conversions happen.

**Hour 3-4:** post the first social proof. As soon as you have 5+ buyers, screenshot the Gumroad sales count and tweet *"Day 1 already 23 sold — thank you all. 48 hours left at $9."*

### T+1 to T+3 — momentum

- **Daily Twitter:** post a single tactic from the book per day. Each tweet ends "Full system in the ebook → [link]"
- **Daily Telegram:** quick value-rich post + discrete reminder
- **LinkedIn (T+2):** a follow-up with reader testimonials (collect them via DM)
- **Email (T+2):** reminder *"36 hours left at $9, then $19 forever"*

### T+4 to T+7 — full price + social proof

- **Email (T+4):** announce the price has gone to $19 + 3 reader testimonials
- **Twitter (T+4 to T+7):** pivot to value-only posts, occasional CTA in replies
- **LinkedIn (T+5):** a "what people said about the book" post

After T+7, transition to evergreen.

### Realistic conversion math

With your channels:

- **Email (6k subs, 30% open, 5% click):** ~90 link clicks, ~5-15% conversion = **5-15 sales**
- **Twitter (4k followers, 5% see launch tweet):** ~200 impressions of buy-link tweet, ~1% click → 2 sales (low because Twitter)
- **LinkedIn (11k, 5-10% see post, 1-2% click):** ~80-200 link clicks, ~3-5% conversion = **3-10 sales**
- **Telegram (7k, 60% open):** ~4,200 see message, ~3-5% click → 200 clicks → ~5% buy = **10-20 sales**
- **Website (2k/mo, daily banner over 7 days):** ~500 visitors see banner, ~5% click → 25 clicks → ~10% buy (warm) = **2-5 sales**

**Total launch:** ~25-65 sales × $9 = **$225-$585** in launch-week revenue at the discounted price.

Then ongoing at $19, you'll see 1-3 organic sales per day from evergreen channels = **$30-$90/day = $900-$2,700/month**.

In 90 days: **$3,000-$8,500 baseline**, more if a tweet or LinkedIn post goes viral.

## Post-launch evergreen

The launch is the *spike*. The *flywheel* is what generates sustained revenue.

### Permanent placements

1. **VisaJobs.xyz footer banner** — every page links to the book
2. **Job listing CTA** — at the bottom of every job page on the site, a small "Get the playbook" CTA
3. **Email signature** — on every Mailchimp newsletter, a permanent link
4. **Telegram pinned message** — permanently pinned with the link

### Weekly content calendar

- **Monday Telegram:** 1 actionable tip (from the book) + soft CTA
- **Wednesday Twitter thread:** a 5-tweet teaser of one chapter, link at the end
- **Friday LinkedIn post:** longer-form value with the buy link in comments

This rhythm reinforces the book without seeming spammy. Aim for **80% value, 20% sell**.

### Affiliate program (after first 100 sales)

Open an affiliate program on Gumroad at 30% commission. Recruit:
- Other tech career creators on YouTube/Twitter
- University career-services offices abroad (often interested in resources for international students)
- Bootcamp alumni networks

Each affiliate sends a few sales/month. Compounds over time.

### Updates as a moat

You promised free updates for life — that's actually a marketing asset:

- Every 6 months, release a "v1.1," "v1.2" update with refreshed visa thresholds and sponsor lists
- Email all past buyers when an update drops — high re-engagement, often resulting in word-of-mouth referrals
- Posts about updates ("just dropped v2 of the playbook with refreshed 2026 numbers") give you legitimate launch-style content multiple times a year

## Pricing experiments (after first 100 sales)

Once you have data:

1. **Test $24 vs $19** for 2 weeks. Most ebooks underprice; small bumps barely affect conversion.
2. **Bundle test:** create a "$49 founder package" with 30-min 1:1 consult. Even at low-volume, the unit economics are excellent.
3. **PPP pricing:** Gumroad supports purchasing power parity discounts — a $19 book becomes $7 in India, $11 in Brazil. Major moral and practical win.

## A note on protecting the bundle

Gumroad's PDF is downloadable, which means it *will* be pirated — every digital product is. Don't worry about it. Trying to DRM it ruins the buyer experience for the 99% of honest customers.

What you *should* do:

- **Watermark each PDF** with the buyer's email at sale time (Gumroad supports this; it's a built-in feature)
- **No silly password protection** — increases friction, doesn't stop piracy
- **Free updates as the real moat** — pirates don't get the updates
